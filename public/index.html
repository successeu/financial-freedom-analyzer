03 Your Income & Expenses

The SpeedWealth Principle: SYSTEMIZE
The first step to building wealth is understanding money flow. You cannot create a scalable system without knowing exactly what comes in and goes out each month.

Enter the total amount earned before taxes from all sources: employment, freelance work, side business, investments, rental income, etc.

Sum of all monthly spending: rent/mortgage, utilities, groceries, transportation, insurance, debt payments, subscriptions, and other recurring costs.

💡 Did you know? If you have a $1,500 monthly surplus, that's $18,000/year—enough to build significant wealth in just a few years through compound growth and smart investing.

04 Your Current Position

The SpeedWealth Principle: START WITH THE END IN MIND
Your current savings and debt are your starting point, not your destination. Understanding them clearly helps you create a realistic roadmap to your wealth vision.

Total accessible savings: emergency fund, savings account, money market account, cash reserves—anything you can access within 30 days.

Combined balance of all debts: credit cards, personal loans, student loans, car loans, mortgage, or any other borrowing.

📊 This matters because: Your debt-to-savings ratio determines your strategy. High debt? Focus on debt reduction first. Low debt? Accelerate investments. Both are valid paths.
